Biography

My name is Joseph Miller and I have been a professional actor since graduating from East 15 School of Acting in September 2013. I love acting, singing, playing the piano and Newcastle United.

I have been involved with theatre since a young age, with my first role being a Dormouse in Alice in Wonderland when I was seven years old, at my local youth theatre, Stagecoach, with Artistic Director John Cooper. From then on I have always had some acting on the go, even whilst taking a break to do a Computer Science degree at Newcastle University, useless for acting but very useful for building websites, before enrolling on the MA Acting course at East 15, run by the masterful Andrea Brooks.

Whilst at East 15, I performed the roles of Trofimov in The Cherry Orchard, Slim in Cowboy Mouth, Romeo in Romeo and Juliet and the Road-Worker in The Plebeians Rehearse The Uprising, directed by David Mercatali. My first role out of East 15 was Gaveston in Edward II, performed in Leeds and directed by Paul Ratcliffe.
